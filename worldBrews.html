COFFEE AROUND THE WORLD

Discover unique coffee traditions from every corner of the globe

Asia-Pacific

Vietnamese Ca Phe
Strong drip coffee served over sweetened condensed milk.
Malaysian Kopi
strong, aromatic coffee brewed with robusta beans, often served with condensed milk or sugar.
Australian Flat White
A Viennese speciality coffee, similar to a cappuccino but with whipped cream instead of milk foam.
Philippine Kapeng Barako
Strong, bold coffee made from Liberica beans, known for its distinctively powerful flavor and aroma.

Europe

Italian Espresso
A concentrated coffee brewed by forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee beans, served in small amounts.
French Café au Lait
Coffee with hot milk added, traditionally served in a wide-bowled cup, perfect for breakfast.
Spanish Cortado
A balanced coffee drink made with equal parts espresso and warm milk.
Greek Frappe
A foam-covered iced coffee drink made from instant coffee, water, sugar, and milk, shaken to create a creamy texture.

America

Cuban Cafecito
A sweet Cuban espresso drink made with sugar whipped with the first drops of espresso.
Mexican Café de Olla
Traditional Mexican coffee brewed with cinnamon and piloncillo in a clay pot.
Brazilian Cafezinho
A small, strong, and sweet coffee, similar to espresso but with a unique Brazilian twist.
Colombian Tinto
A simple black coffee, often served in small cups, made from high-quality Colombian beans.

Africa & Middle East

Ethiopian Coffee
A traditional ritual of roasting, grinding, and brewing coffee, often taking several hours to complete.
Arabic Qahwa
A traditional Arabic coffee flavored with cardamom and sometimes saffron, served in small cups.
Moroccan Café Noir
Strong black coffee often served with a side of dates or sweet pastries in Moroccan cafes.
Turkish Coffee
Finely ground coffee brewed in a cezve, served unfiltered with grounds settled at the bottom.
